Integrating in spherical coordinates, the differential element is `sin theta d theta d phi`.

E.g. the area of hemisphere: `int_0^(pi/2) int_0^(2pi) |sin theta| d theta d phi = 2pi`

This explains why...
Theorem:The lambertian BRDF with unit reflectance is `1/pi`.

Proof: Assume lambertian surface with unit reflectance lit uniformly from all directions. Hemispherical-directional reflectance `rho_hd` is the same for all directions. Because unit reflectance, it must be equal to 1.

`rho_(hd) (omega_o) = int_(fr "H"^2) f_r(p,omega_o,omega_i)|cos theta_i| d omega_i = 1`
`= int_o^(pi/2) int_0^(2pi) f_r(p,omega_o,omega_i)|cos theta_i sin theta_i| d theta_i d phi_i `
Since `f_r` is constant move it outside...
`= f_r(p,omega_o,omega_i) int_o^(pi/2) int_0^(2pi) |cos theta_i sin theta_i| d theta_i d phi_i `
The integeral resolves to `pi` (Wolfram Alpha)
`= f_r(p,omega_o,omega_i) pi = 1`
`<=> f_r(p,omega_o,omega_i) = 1/pi`
